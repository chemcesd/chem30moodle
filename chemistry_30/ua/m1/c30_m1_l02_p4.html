Practice 4.

  1. If the mass of material undergoing temperature change is doubled and the other variables remain the same, then the thermal energy is doubled.

  2. If the specific heat capacity of material undergoing temperature change is divided by two (is halved) and the other variables remain the same, then the thermal energy is halved.

  3. If the temperature change of the material is tripled and the other variables remain the same, then the thermal energy is tripled.

  4. If all three variables (m, c, and Δt) are doubled, then the thermal energy is increased by a factor of 8 (2 × 2 × 2 = 8).
