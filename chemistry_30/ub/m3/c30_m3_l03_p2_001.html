SC 2.

 

Practice 26.

 

a.

 

Species List

Zn(s), H+(aq), Cl–(aq), H2O(l)

Oxidizing Agents

H+(aq), H2O(l)  

Strongest OA = H+(aq)

Reducing Agents

Zn(s), Cl–(aq), H2O(l), Cl–(aq) and H2O(l)
Strongest RA = Zn(s)

Reduction Half-Reaction

2 H+ (aq) + 2 e– → H2(g)

Oxidation Half-Reaction

Zn(s) → Zn2+(aq) + 2 e–

Net Equation

2 H+ (aq) + Zn(s) → H2(g) + Zn2+(aq)

The reaction is spontaneous since the position of the SOA is above the SRA in the table.

Diagnostic Tests

For hydrogen, a “pop” test. A “pop” sound is heard when a flame is inserted into the gas produced by the reaction.

 

Measure a change in pH before and after the reaction. pH is expected to increase due to the decrease in the concentration of available hydrogen ions that are used in the reaction.

 

b.

 


Species List

Au(s), H+(aq), Cl–(aq), H2O(l)

Oxidizing Agents

H+(aq), H2O(l)  

Strongest OA = H+(aq)

Reducing Agents

Au(s), Cl–(aq), H2O(l), Cl–(aq), and H2O(l)
Strongest RA = H2O(l)

Reduction Half-Reaction

 2 H+ (aq) + 2 e– → H2(g)

Oxidation Half-Reaction

2 H2O(l)→ O2(g) + 4 H+(aq) + 4 e–

Net Equation

 2 H2O(l)→ 2 H2(g) + O2(g)

The reaction is non-spontaneous since the position of the SOA is below the SRA in the table.

Diagnostic Tests

No change in observable properties.

 

c.

 

Species List

H+(aq), NO3–(aq), Cu(s), H2O(l)

Oxidizing Agents

H+(aq), NO3–(aq), H2O(l)  
Strongest OA = H+(aq) AND NO3–(aq)

Reducing Agents

Cu(s), H2O(l)
Strongest RA = Cu(s)

Reduction Half-Reaction

2 NO3–(aq) + 4 H+ (aq) + 2 e– → N2O4(g) + 2 H2O(l)

Oxidation Half-Reaction

Cu(s) → Cu2+(aq) + 2 e–

Net Equation

2 NO3–(aq) + 4 H+ (aq) + Cu(s) → N2O4(g) + 2 H2O(l) + Cu2+(aq)

The reaction is spontaneous since the position of the SOA is above the SRA in the table.

Diagnostic Tests

Observe colour change as the reaction proceeds. A blue colour should develop as aqueous copper(II) ions are produced by the reaction. This could be confirmed by a blue colour resulting from a flame test of the solution containing the products of the reaction.
