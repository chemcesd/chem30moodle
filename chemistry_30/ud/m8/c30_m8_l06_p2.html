Practice 1.

  1. The slope of the buffering region is relatively flat (slope = nearly 0).
  1. The buffering region is where the concentration of the reactant in the test solution is reducing as a result of being converted chemically due to the addition of titrant.
  1. Near the equivalence point, a rapid change in pH occurs; therefore, the slope of the graph is very large.
  1. Nearing the equivalence point, the chemical amount of titrant added is almost equal to the chemical amount of reactant in the test solution. The dramatic change in pH witnessed is a direct result of the chemical conversion of the reactant in the test solution and its decreased influence on the properties of the solution. Past the equivalence point, titrant is in excess and the pH change is due to the accumulation of titrant and its influence on the properties of the resulting solution.
